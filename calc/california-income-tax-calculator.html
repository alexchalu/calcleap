California Standard Deduction 2026

California offers a standard deduction that reduces your taxable income:

  • Single: $5,363
  • Married Filing Jointly: $10,726
  • Head of Household: $10,726

These deductions are significantly lower than federal standard deductions, resulting in more taxable income at the state level.

Who Pays the Most CA Income Tax?

California's progressive tax structure means high earners bear most of the burden:

  • Top 1% of earners (income > $800,000) pay ~50% of all CA income tax
  • Earners over $1 million face a 13.3% marginal rate on top income
  • Middle-class earners ($50k-$150k) pay 6-9.3% on most income
  • Low earners (< $30k) pay 1-4%, often offset by credits

California Tax Credits and Deductions

California offers several credits to reduce your tax bill:

  • Earned Income Tax Credit (CA EITC) — Up to $3,417 for low-income workers
  • Young Child Tax Credit — $1,117 per child under 6 (for EITC recipients)
  • Dependent Exemption Credit — $446 per dependent
  • Renter's Credit — $60-120 for low-income renters
  • Mortgage Interest Deduction — Up to $1 million in mortgage debt (state level)
  • Charitable Contributions — Itemized deductions available

When Are California Taxes Due?

California tax returns are due April 15th (or the next business day if it falls on a weekend/holiday), matching the federal deadline.

Extension: You can file for an automatic 6-month extension to October 15th. However, you must still pay any estimated tax owed by April 15th to avoid penalties.

Estimated tax payments: If you're self-employed or have significant non-wage income, you must make quarterly estimated tax payments (April 15, June 15, September 15, January 15).

Frequently Asked Questions

What is California's income tax rate?
California has progressive tax rates from 1% to 13.3%. Most middle-class earners pay 6-9.3% on the majority of their income. High earners (over $1 million) pay the nation's highest state income tax rate at 13.3%.
Do I have to pay both CA state tax and federal tax?
Yes. California residents pay both federal income tax (to the IRS) and California state income tax (to the Franchise Tax Board). You file two separate tax returns. Federal taxes cannot be deducted on your CA return, but state taxes can be deducted on your federal return (up to $10,000 SALT cap).
How much is California state tax on $100,000?
For a single filer earning $100,000, California state income tax is approximately $4,800-5,000 (effective rate ~5%). This doesn't include federal tax or FICA.
Does California tax Social Security or retirement income?
California does NOT tax Social Security benefits. However, most other retirement income (401k withdrawals, IRA distributions, pensions) is fully taxable at California's normal income tax rates.
Can I claim the federal standard deduction on my CA return?
No. California has its own, much lower standard deduction ($5,363 single vs. $14,600 federal in 2026). This means more of your income is taxable at the state level.
What happens if I move to/from California mid-year?
You file as a part-year resident and only pay CA tax on income earned while living in California. You'll need to prorate your income and deductions. California is aggressive about taxing former residents on California-source income (e.g., stock options from a CA employer).
Are capital gains taxed differently in California?
No. California taxes capital gains (stocks, crypto, real estate) as ordinary income at the same 1-13.3% rates. There's no special lower rate like at the federal level, making CA expensive for investors.
How can I reduce my California tax bill?
Maximize retirement contributions (401k, IRA), claim all eligible credits (EITC, dependent credits), itemize deductions if beneficial, consider tax-loss harvesting for investments, and if self-employed, deduct all business expenses. High earners sometimes relocate to no-tax states like Nevada or Texas to avoid CA's 13.3% rate.
