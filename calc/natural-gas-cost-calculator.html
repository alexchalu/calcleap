Understanding Natural Gas Costs

Natural gas is measured in therms (100 cubic feet or ~100,000 BTUs). The national average price is around $1.20-$1.50 per therm, but it varies widely by region and season. Urban areas with pipeline access typically pay less than rural areas requiring delivery.

Your natural gas bill has two main components:

  • Usage charges — based on the number of therms you consume
  • Fixed charges — monthly service fees, delivery charges, and taxes (typically $10-25/month)

The calculator above focuses on usage charges. Add your utility's monthly service fee to get your total bill estimate.

Factors That Affect Natural Gas Costs

  • Climate — Cold climates use 3-4x more gas for heating than mild climates
  • Home insulation — Poor insulation can double your heating costs
  • Furnace efficiency — AFUE ratings range from 80% (older) to 98% (new high-efficiency)
  • Thermostat settings — Each degree lower saves ~3% on heating costs
  • Regional prices — Natural gas prices vary from $0.80/therm (Texas) to $2.50/therm (Hawaii)
  • Seasonal demand — Winter prices can be 50-100% higher due to heating demand
  • Appliance age — Older appliances waste 10-30% more gas

How to Reduce Natural Gas Costs

  • Upgrade to a high-efficiency furnace (95%+ AFUE) — saves $200-500/year
  • Lower your thermostat by 1-2°F — saves 3-6% on heating
  • Seal air leaks around windows, doors, and ducts — reduces waste by 10-20%
  • Install a programmable thermostat — automatically lower heat when you're asleep or away
  • Insulate your attic and walls — can cut heating costs by 20-30%
  • Maintain your furnace — annual tune-ups improve efficiency by 5-10%
  • Use a tankless water heater — 20-30% more efficient than traditional tanks
  • Close vents in unused rooms — redirect heat to frequently used spaces

Regional Price Variations

Natural gas prices vary dramatically by state. Here are average residential prices (per therm):

  • Lowest: Texas ($0.80-1.00), Louisiana ($0.85-1.05), Oklahoma ($0.90-1.10)
  • National Average: $1.20-1.50
  • Highest: Hawaii ($2.50-3.00), Alaska ($1.80-2.20), New England ($1.60-2.00)

Proximity to production areas (like Texas and the Gulf Coast) results in lower prices. Remote regions and areas without pipeline access pay significantly more.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the average natural gas bill?
The average U.S. household spends $600-900 per year on natural gas, or $50-75/month. However, this varies widely: mild climates average $30-40/month, while cold climates can average $100-150/month in winter.
How many therms does the average home use per month?
The national average is 60-80 therms/month, but this varies dramatically by season and climate. Summer usage (water heater, stove) is typically 20-30 therms/month. Winter usage with heating can spike to 100-200 therms/month in cold climates.
Is natural gas cheaper than electricity for heating?
In most regions, yes. Natural gas heating costs $12-15 per million BTU, while electric resistance heating costs $35-50. However, electric heat pumps (which are 2-3x more efficient) can be cost-competitive with natural gas in mild climates.
How can I tell if my natural gas bill is too high?
Compare your therms per square foot. A well-insulated home in a moderate climate should use 0.04-0.06 therms/sq ft/month in summer and 0.3-0.5 therms/sq ft/month in winter. If you're using significantly more, check for leaks, poor insulation, or inefficient appliances.
What is a therm of natural gas?
A therm is 100,000 BTUs of energy, equivalent to about 100 cubic feet (CCF) of natural gas. Utilities bill in either therms or CCF — they're roughly equivalent for billing purposes.
Why is my natural gas bill higher in winter?
Heating accounts for 60-70% of residential natural gas use. In cold climates, winter gas usage can be 4-6x higher than summer usage, as your furnace runs constantly to maintain indoor temperature.
Should I lock in a natural gas rate?
Fixed-rate plans can protect against price spikes but may cost more if prices drop. If you're in a deregulated market and prices are currently low, locking in for 12-24 months can provide stability. Always compare the fixed rate to your utility's current variable rate.
How much does it cost to heat a house with natural gas?
For a 1,500 sq ft home in a moderate climate, expect $400-700/year for heating (450-600 therms @ $1.20/therm). Cold climates can cost $800-1,200/year. Large homes (3,000+ sq ft) in cold climates can exceed $1,500/year.
